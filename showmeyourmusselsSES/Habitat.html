Wild mussels spend their lives partially buried in river and lake bottoms, where they can be mistaken for rocks.
Most people are not aware of the many different kinds of animals that live under the water of our lakes and streams.
Freshwater mussels inhabit lakes and rivers throughout the world. They reach their greatest diversity in North America,
where about 300 species are found. They often live in multi-species communities called mussel beds. These beds can support 30 or more species.
